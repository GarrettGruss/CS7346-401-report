# Architectural Katas – Presentation Phase
## Presentation Overview
The Presentation Phase is the culminating stage of the Architectural Katas project. During this phase, project teams will either present their proposed cloud architecture or participate as reviewers for other teams’ presentations.

This phase is designed to simulate a professional architecture review, where teams must clearly communicate and defend their architectural decisions, assumptions, and tradeoffs to a technical audience.

## Presentation Format and Participation
If You Are Presenting
Your team will present a high-level architectural vision of your proposed cloud-native solution.

- **Time limit**: Your presentation must be no more than 15 minutes total, including Q&A.
- **Focus**: The presentation should summarize and synthesize your written report. You are not expected to cover every section of the report in equal detail. Instead, focus on the most important architectural decisions, assumptions, and tradeoffs, and be prepared to address additional details during Q&A.
- **Audience**: Assume your audience consists of technically informed peers who may ask questions about design choices, risks, scalability, cost considerations, and framework alignment.

## If You Are Not Presenting
If your team is not presenting during a given session, you are expected to:

- Actively listen to other teams’ presentations
- Ask thoughtful, relevant questions during the Q&A portion
- Engage critically with architectural decisions, assumptions, and tradeoffs
Active participation as a reviewer is a required component of this phase.

## Required Presentation Content (High-Level)
Your presentation should address the following topics at a high level:

- Kata Overview: Problem domain and key requirements
- Architectural Vision: Overall cloud-based design approach
- Key Assumptions and Constraints: Assumptions that significantly shaped the architecture
- System Architecture Diagram: Major components and data flows
- Cloud Architecture Quality Attributes (“-ilities”): Most important quality attributes and how they are prioritized
- Cloud-Native Design Considerations: Selected Twelve-Factor App principles relevant to the solution
- Well-Architected Framework Alignment: Key pillars emphasized and tradeoffs made
- Cloud Services Selection: Major services used and high-level justification
- Scalability, Availability, and Fault Tolerance: How the system scales and handles failures
- Cost Considerations: Major cost drivers and cost-related tradeoffs
- Risks and Mitigation Strategies: Most significant risks and how they are mitigated
- Conclusion: Strengths, limitations, and potential future improvements

## Presentation Deliverable
Each team must submit a professional presentation deck (PowerPoint) to Canvas by the due date.

Slides should be:
- clear and readable,
- visually organized,
- focused on key ideas rather than dense text.
Slides are expected to support your spoken explanation, not replace it.

## Presentation Style and Accessibility
- Presenters may use speaker notes or brief reference notes during the presentation.
- Reading directly from slides or a script is discouraged but not prohibited.
- Presentations are evaluated based on clarity of explanation and architectural reasoning.
- Slides should contain concise prompts, diagrams, and visuals that support explanation.
Most successful presentations use approximately 10–12 slides, including the title and conclusion.

## Important Notes
- This is a design defense and communication exercise, not an implementation or deployment demonstration.
- You are not required to build or deploy the system.
- There is no single correct solution.
- Architectural reasoning, justification of decisions, and defensible tradeoffs are the primary evaluation criteria.
For best results, refer to the presentation rubric while preparing your slides.
