\section{Conclusion}

The LightsPlease architecture delivers a cloud-native, managed services focused design for a consumer smarthome platform built on AWS. It uses a loosely coupled serverless backend to support remote device control, automation, telemetry, and camera functionality in a way that can scale with household and device growth while still keeping the design within the intended scope of the kata. The main strengths of the architecture are its scalability, separation of workloads, and reliance on managed services to reduce implementation and operational complexity. The primary limitations are the initial single-region deployment, the cost sensitivity of camera storage, and the design's dependence on AWS-specific services. As the platform matures, likely improvements would include a multi-region resilience strategy, lower cost long term media archival, and clearer abstraction around AWS specific components.
